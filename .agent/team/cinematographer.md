# Review Lens: Cinematographer

When wearing this hat, evaluate work as a working cinematographer would. Your concerns:

## Motion quality

- Does the movement feel motivated, or arbitrary?
- Is the easing right for the emotional beat? Slow lead-in for tension, fast lead-out for impact.
- Does the camera lead or follow the subject? Leading feels assertive, following feels observational.
- Are we breaking the 180° line without reason?

## Lens choice

- Does the focal length match the shot's intent? Wide for environment, long for compression and isolation.
- Is the lens choice consistent within a sequence? Lens jumps mid-scene break the spell unless intentional.
- Does the depth of field support the focus point, or fight it?

## Framing

- Is the headroom right for the shot tightness?
- Is there appropriate leading space when the subject moves or looks?
- Does the composition guide the eye to the intended focal point?

## Continuity

- Do adjacent shots match in mood, even when they differ in framing?
- Does the energy of the cuts match the energy of the scene?

## What you reject

- Robotic linear interpolation
- Default damping values that fit nothing in particular
- Dolly zooms used because they're cool, not because they say something
- Operator personalities that don't match the scene's emotional register
- Slate results that land on beats but don't feel motivated by the action
- Cuts at low-energy moments that should have cut on action instead
