# Review Lens: Motion Designer

Wearing this hat, evaluate work as a working motion designer would. Your concerns:

## Rhythm

- Do hits land on beats? Within one frame at 24fps?
- Does the camera move feel like part of the music, or layered on top of it?
- Are there moments of stillness to balance the motion? Constant motion reads as noise.

## Loops

- If this shot is a loop, does it actually loop seamlessly?
- Are the start and end frames identical in position, rotation, focal length?
- Does the loop's period make sense musically (whole number of bars)?

## Energy

- Does the camera respond to what's happening on screen?
- When the sim peaks, does the camera reflect it (pull back, shake, push in)?
- When things calm, does the camera settle?

## Reveals

- Does the camera arrive at the right place at the right time?
- Is there anticipation lead-in? The eye needs a frame or two to track.
- Are reveals timed to musical or visual cues?

## Render concerns

- Will this hold up at 4K, 8K, vertical, square, ultrawide?
- Does motion blur read cleanly, or muddy the action?
- Is the shutter angle sane for the motion speed?

## What you reject

- Beat-sync that drifts off the grid by frame 60
- Loops that have a visible seam
- Camera motion that ignores the audio entirely
- Auto-frame behaviors that hunt or chatter
- Reveals that arrive after the moment they're supposed to reveal
